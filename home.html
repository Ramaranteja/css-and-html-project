FRESH FRUITS

Organic fruits are grown without the use of synthetic pesticides, chemical fertilizers, or genetically modified organisms (GMOs). Instead, organic farming relies on natural methods such as composting, crop rotation, and biological pest control to maintain healthy soil and minimize environmental impact. This approach prioritizes sustainability and biodiversity, ensuring fruits are cultivated in harmony with nature.

MANGOES

Mango (Mangifera indica), often called the "king of fruits," is a tropical fruit known for its juicy, sweet, and aromatic flesh.

APPLES

Apple (Malus domestica) is a popular and widely cultivated fruit known for its crisp texture, sweet-tart flavor,

BANANA

Banana (Musa spp.) is a widely consumed tropical fruit known for its soft, sweet, and creamy flesh. Typically yellow when ripe.

Organic vegetables are grown without the use of synthetic pesticides, herbicides, or chemical fertilizers, ensuring they are free from harmful residues. These vegetables are cultivated through sustainable farming practices that promote soil health, biodiversity, and ecological balance. Organic farming avoids genetically modified organisms (GMOs)

FRESH VEGETABLES

LEAFY VEGETABLES

Leafy vegetables, also known as greens or leafy greens, are plant leaves consumed as vegetables. They are typically rich in nutrients

ROOT VEGETABLES

Root vegetables are edible plants where the root or underground part is consumed as food. These vegetables store nutrients in their roots

BULB VEGETABLES

Bulb vegetables are plants in which the bulb, or the underground storage organ, is the edible part. These vegetables store nutrients in the bulb

DRY FRUITS

Dry fruits are naturally or artificially dehydrated fruits that have had most of their water content removed while retaining their nutritional value. This drying process concentrates the flavors and nutrients, making them a rich source of energy, vitamins, minerals, and fiber.

ALMONDS

Almonds are nutrient-dense, edible seeds of the almond tree (Prunus dulcis). They are among the most popular nuts consumed worldwide

CASHEW

Cashew nuts are the edible seeds of the cashew tree (Anacardium occidentale), native to tropical regions. Known for their creamy texture and slightly sweet

DATES

Dates are the sweet, chewy fruits of the date palm tree (Phoenix dactylifera), commonly grown in the Middle East, North Africa, and parts of Asia.
